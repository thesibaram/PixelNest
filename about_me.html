I'm from Sanakholi, which is close to Asika, and I attend Parala Maharaja Engineering College to study electrical engineering. Even though my phone has a very good camera, I don't spend much time taking pictures myself. My phone's gallery is filled with pictures of other people, and I don't mind because I have 512GB of storage space. However, there are times when I enjoy taking pictures, such as when I'm travelling, doing something interesting, or while coding, among many other things.

I created PixelNest, a straightforward, useful space, to preserve those memories and make them accessible. It is intended to be a personal archive rather than a professional portfolio. For fun and personality, the site leans slightly towards a comic-inspired aesthetic, but underneath it all, I've tried to keep the design simple, quick, and image-focused.

Respect and privacy are important. I try to be careful about how many photos depict people and locations I've been to. This website is essentially a personal, digital memory book that is enjoyable to peruse and a neat place to revisit significant moments.
